### [1] [Forward trijet production in proton-nucleus collisions: gluon initiated channel](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07509)
*Paul Caucal,Marcos Guerrero Morales,Farid Salazar*

Main category: hep-ph

TL;DR: The paper presents calculations of the forward trijet production differential cross section in the gluon initiated channel using the Color Glass Condensate framework, highlighting the role of gluon interactions and divergence structures critical for validating the dilute-dense hybrid formalism.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To explore gluon dynamics at small-x in proton-nucleus collisions by computing differential cross sections, validate the effectiveness of the CGC formalism, and pave the way for next-leading order calculations.

Method: Uses the CGC effective theory with covariant perturbation theory, splitting amplitudes into regular/instantaneous parts. Focuses on gluon-initiated processes (q q̄ g and ggg final states) and analyzes momentum correlations via multiparton color correlators and impact factors.

Result: Identified four-gluon vertex behavior similar to instantaneous contributions, showed rapidity divergence ties to JIMWLK evolution, and collinear divergences link to PDF/fragmentation functions. Validated dilute-dense hybrid formalism at NLO.

Conclusion: Demonstrates CGC's capability in handling complex gluon interactions, confirms divergence structure expectations, and provides necessary components for higher-order calculations in proton-nucleus scattering studies.

Abstract: In this paper, we present the results for the forward trijet production differential cross section in the gluon initiated channel at leading order in proton-nucleus collisions. The calculations are carried out within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, and in the dilute-dense approximation, using effective vertices for the quark and gluon propagators interacting with the small-$x$ background gluon field. We employ the covariant perturbation theory approach and disentangle the amplitudes into regular and instantaneous contributions. Our results are expressed as convolutions of multiparton color correlators of light-like Wilson lines and perturbative impact factors, organized in compact expressions in terms of the ``bare" topologies of the contributing diagrams. The gluon initiated channel receives contributions from a $q\bar{q}g$ and a $ggg$ final state. Interestingly, when considering the $ggg$ final state, we observe, for the first time, that the four-gluon vertex topology follows a structure similar to the instantaneous contributions. Furthermore, when integrating (one of) the real gluon(s) in the final state, we identify that: i) the rapidity divergence contributes to the real part of JIMWLK of the leading-order color correlator; and ii) the collinear divergence contribute to the evolution of initial-state gluon parton distribution function, and final state fragmentation functions. These results validate the dilute-dense hybrid formalism at one-loop order, and are key ingredients towards the complete next-to-leading order calculation of dijet/dihadron production in proton--nucleus collisions.

### [2] [Transverse energy-momentum tensor distributions in polarized nucleons](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07616)
*Ho-Yeon Won,Cédric Lorcé*

Main category: hep-ph

TL;DR: This paper investigates the relativistic spatial distributions of the energy-momentum tensor within polarized nucleons using a quantum phase-space formalism, focusing on components with transverse indices and their multipole structure in a moving nucleon. It connects the framework to standard light-front distributions in the infinite-momentum frame.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To understand the relativistic spatial distribution of energy-momentum in polarized nucleons and reconcile findings with established light-front quantization approaches, particularly addressing components involving transverse indices and their multipole decomposition in a moving frame.

Method: The study employs the quantum phase-space formalism to analyze transverse components of the energy-momentum tensor. It examines multipole expansions of these distributions in a moving nucleon and derives their correspondence with light-front distributions in the infinite-momentum limit.

Result: The formalism successfully reproduces standard light-front distributions, including those with 'bad' components, and provides insight into their structural origins through the analysis of transverse coordinates and multipole contributions.

Conclusion: The quantum phase-space approach offers a comprehensive framework for interpreting relativistic nucleon energy-momentum distributions, clarifying the connection between transverse spatial structures and light-front dynamics.

Abstract: We complete our study of the relativistic spatial distributions of the energy-momentum tensor inside polarized nucleons within the quantum phase-space formalism. In the present work, we focus on the components of the energy-momentum tensor involving at least one transverse index. We also explore the multipole structure of the transverse distributions in a moving nucleon. In the infinite-momentum frame, we show that the formalism reproduces the standard light-front distributions, including those with a ``bad'' component, and explains the origin of their structure.

</details>
