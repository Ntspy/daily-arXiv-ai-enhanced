### [1] [Prying Open the Dark Sector Window with SBND Off-Target Mode](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25818)
*Bhaskar Dutta,Debopam Goswami,Aparajitha Karthikeyan,Vishvas Pandey,Zahra Tabrizi,Adrian Thompson,Richard G. Van de Water*

Main category: hep-ph

TL;DR: The paper presents an analysis of the physics potential of operating the Short-Baseline Near Detector (SBND) at Fermilab in two novel configurations—BNB off-target and dedicated beam-dump modes—to enhance sensitivity to new physics scenarios like light dark matter, axion-like particles, and heavy neutral leptons by reducing neutrino backgrounds.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to leverage advanced detector technologies and beam configurations to suppress neutrino-induced backgrounds, thereby improving SBND's ability to detect signals from beyond the Standard Model physics such as sub-GeV dark matter and other exotic particles.

Method: The study explores running SBND in BNB off-target and beam-dump configurations, where proton beams are directed away from the standard target or use a dedicated dump, reducing background neutrinos. Projected sensitivities are calculated for various new physics scenarios using these setups.

Result: The analysis shows that these configurations significantly extend SBND's reach for detecting new physics, with improved sensitivities to light dark matter, axion-like particles, heavy neutral leptons, and meson-portal models compared to traditional beam setups.

Conclusion: Implementing these beam configurations represents a promising strategy to enhance SBND's capabilities in probing beyond Standard Model physics, offering competitive constraints on sub-GeV scale particles and interactions.

Abstract: Accelerator-based neutrino experiments with high-intensity proton beams and advanced detector technologies provide a powerful and complementary approach to probing physics beyond the Standard Model. The MiniBooNE experiment at Fermilab pioneered a dedicated Booster Neutrino Beam (BNB) off-target (beam-dump) run, setting leading constraints on sub-GeV dark matter. In this work, we explore the physics opportunities enabled by operating the Short-Baseline Near Detector (SBND) at Fermilab in a future BNB off-target configuration, as well as in a dedicated beam-dump configuration. By redirecting the proton beam away from the nominal beryllium target, or by employing a dedicated beam-dump, neutrino-induced backgrounds are substantially suppressed, thereby enhancing SBND's sensitivity to many new physics scenarios. We demonstrate that such running modes significantly extend the reach for new physics. As representative examples, we present projected sensitivities to light dark matter, axion-like particles, heavy neutral leptons, and meson-portal scenarios.

### [2] [(3+1)D dilute Glasma initial conditions in simulations of heavy-ion collisions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.26331)
*Kayran Schmidt*

Main category: hep-ph

TL;DR: The paper presents a (3+1)D approximation of Glasma dynamics breaking boost-invariance through linearized Yang-Mills equations, deriving analytic solutions and a generalized nuclear model. Key findings include rapidity dependence, universal parametrizations of gluon distributions, and limiting fragmentation at high rapidities.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To address limitations of boost-invariant models in describing longitudinal dynamics of the Glasma and explore non-equilibrium QCD phenomena in heavy-ion collisions using a more realistic 3D framework.

Method: Linearization of Yang-Mills equations in covariant gauge, development of a 3D McLerran-Venugopalan model with realistic nuclear envelopes, analytic solutions in position/momentum space, and numerical evaluation of energy-momentum tensor, gluon distribution, and transverse energy.

Result: Derived analytic solutions showing boost-invariance breaking via longitudinal collision geometry, universal parametrizations of momentum-space distributions with two scaling parameters, and limiting fragmentation observed in rapidity profiles at large rapidities.

Conclusion: The 3D dilute Glasma framework effectively captures non-boost-invariant dynamics, provides physically meaningful parametrizations, and validates limiting fragmentation predictions, offering a robust theoretical tool for studying early-time QCD evolution in heavy-ion collisions.

Abstract: In this thesis, an approximation for the full (3+1)D dynamics of the Glasma is presented, which breaks boost-invariance on the level of the nuclear fields and leads to rapidity dependence in the final results. For this treatment, the Yang-Mills equations are linearized in covariant gauge, where lower-order, nonlinear contributions are neglected and the dynamics are captured by the (3+1)D dilute Glasma. The analytic solutions of the (3+1)D dilute Glasma are derived in both position and momentum space formulations, providing a comprehensive understanding of the involved (3+1)D dynamics. In position space, the field strength tensor results from the integration of free-streaming gluons that are produced in $2\rightarrow1$ scattering processes where the initial nuclear fields overlap. In momentum space, the event-averaged gluon number distribution for the (3+1)D dilute Glasma is derived in Coulomb gauge. A generalized, three-dimensional McLerran-Venugopalan nuclear model is developed for nuclei with realistic envelopes and intrinsic longitudinal correlations. Numerical results are presented for the rapidity structure of the energy-momentum tensor, the gluon number distribution, and the transverse energy of the (3+1)D dilute Glasma. In position space, the extended longitudinal collision geometry and finite longitudinal correlation length break boost-invariance. In momentum space, the results each follow universal parametrizations and are fixed by the values of two scaling parameters. Furthermore, the numerical results exhibit limiting fragmentation where the rapidity profiles approach a limiting distribution at large rapidities. This feature is also derived locally in position space for the analytic expressions of the field strength tensor and, in momentum space, for the transverse energy of the (3+1)D dilute Glasma.

</details>


### [3] [Evaluation of QED cross sections in strong magnetic fields](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.26545)
*Olavi Kiuru*

Main category: hep-ph

TL;DR: This paper calculates scattering cross sections for certain QED processes under extreme magnetic fields exceeding the Schwinger limit, using a formalism similar to magnetic catalysis research, and integrates these calculations into an open-source Python tool for further applications in magnetospheric plasma studies.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To understand the nonlinear effects of QED in ultra-strong magnetic fields found in magnetar magnetospheres, which significantly alter plasma dynamics through modified scattering processes.

Method: The author extends a formalism originally used for magnetic catalysis to compute tree-level QED scattering cross sections (1-to-2, 2-to-1, 2-to-2) without photon propagators under strong magnetic fields, focusing on computational methods formalism.

Result: Derives cross sections for specified processes under such fields, with computational toolkit implementation facilitating further studies on plasma dynamics, deferring detailed astrophysical implications to a companion paper.

Conclusion: The developed framework provides essential tools for modeling magnetospheric environments around magnetars, bridging theoretical QED calculations with practical astrophysical applications through open-source software.

Abstract: Quantum electrodynamics (QED) becomes nonlinear when the magnetic field strength surpasses the critical Schwinger limit $B_Q \approx 4.41\cdot 10^{13}$ G. This limit is surpassed, for example, in the magnetospheres of a specific class of neutron stars known as magnetars, which has important consequences for magnetospheric plasma dynamics due to modifications in scattering cross sections. Using a formalism previously applied to the study of magnetic catalysis, I calculate the cross sections of all tree-level 1-to-2, 2-to-1, and 2-to-2 particle QED scattering processes that do not include a photon propagator. The calculations are done in a strong background magnetic field and the results are implemented into an open-source Python package. This article focuses on presenting the formalism and computational techniques required for the calculations, while the impact of the results on, e.g., magnetospheric plasma dynamics is discussed in a companion letter (Kiuru et al. 2026).

</details>


### [4] [Sensitivity to new physics: single-Higgs couplings vs. the trilinear Higgs coupling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.26633)

Main category: hep-ph

TL;DR: The paper emphasizes the importance of measuring the trilinear Higgs self-coupling to detect beyond Standard Model physics, as certain BSM scenarios predict significant deviations in this coupling without affecting other Higgs observables, which are hard to probe otherwise.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To explore how measuring the trilinear Higgs coupling can provide a unique probe of the Higgs potential structure and electroweak phase transition, and to identify BSM scenarios where deviations in this coupling are significant while other Higgs observables remain SM-like.

Method: Analysis of several BSM scenarios compatible with existing constraints, focusing on models where trilinear coupling deviations are substantial but other Higgs measurements align with SM predictions.

Result: The study identifies BSM models (e.g., extended Higgs sectors) where the trilinear Higgs coupling deviates from SM predictions while other observables remain SM-like, showing the coupling's potential to reveal BSM physics.

Conclusion: A precise measurement of the trilinear Higgs coupling at a future Higgs factory is crucial to validate or rule out these BSM scenarios, underscoring its role as a key probe of new physics beyond the Standard Model.

Abstract: The trilinear Higgs self-coupling provides a unique probe of the structure of the Higgs potential and of the nature of the electroweak phase transition, and constitutes a key target for future collider experiments. Recent studies have shown that confronting theoretical predictions for the trilinear Higgs coupling with current experimental bounds offers a powerful and complementary way to test effects of physics beyond the Standard Model (BSM), in particular those arising from extended Higgs sectors. Meanwhile, substantial progress has been achieved in the precise calculation and automation of the trilinear Higgs coupling in a wide class of BSM models. This contribution discusses several BSM scenarios, compatible with existing constraints, in which sizeable deviations in the trilinear Higgs coupling w.r.t. the Standard Model (SM) value are predicted, while other Higgs observables remain close to their SM expectations and are therefore difficult to probe experimentally. These results highlight the strong physics motivation for a precise measurement of the trilinear Higgs coupling at a future Higgs factory.
